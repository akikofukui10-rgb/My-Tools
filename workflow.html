Reference

Quick-start prompts

PACES · Daily anchor

One vignette, any station

Use before a shift or as a warm-up. Keeps PACES active without a full session.

Give me one PACES-style clinical vignette. Rotate between: Cardiology, Respiratory, Neurology, Geriatrics, Palliative. Start with the scenario and examination findings. Ask me what I'd do next, then wait for my answer before teaching. Flag any management ladder errors I make.
Research · Session start

Define today's deliverable

Use at the start of every research session. Prevents drift and lost momentum.

I'm working on [project name]. Last time I [completed X / got stuck on Y]. Today I have [N hours]. Define one specific deliverable I can finish today. Break it into 4 timed steps. Check in with me after each step.
Writing · Note article

Rough ideas → Japanese draft

Paste your rough ideas and get a full Japanese draft to edit. Your biggest Claude impact area.

テーマ：[topic] ラフなアイデア：[paste ideas in Japanese or English] 読者：30代の日本人医師・医学生 トーン：温かく、個人的で、先輩が正直に振り返るような文体 まず3つの書き出しを提案してください。選んだら全文を書いてください。800字以内でNote向けに。
Portfolio · Batch

Multiple reflections at once

Write 3–4 portfolio entries in one session. Much faster than one at a time.

I need to write [N] IMT portfolio reflections. Here are the clinical encounters: 1. [Brief description of case/encounter] 2. [Brief description] 3. [Brief description] For each: draft a ~100-word reflection covering what I did, what I learned, and reflection on my clinical practice. Number them clearly. Use professional but natural language.
PACES · Management ladder

Framework for a weak topic

Your dominant error type. Build the ladder, not just the facts.

Teach me [topic] as a clinical decision tree. Format: 1. Presenting scenario 2. First-line assessment / investigation 3. Management step 1 → trigger for step 2 → trigger for step 3 4. When to escalate and to what Then give me a vignette and test me. Tell me if I get the sequence wrong, not just the answer.
