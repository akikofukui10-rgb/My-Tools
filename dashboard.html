The Angle — What Makes This Publishable
  • Despite 93% on ART, neuro burden persists — ART necessary but not sufficient
  • Uncontrolled viral load = strongest predictor of neurological presentation in 2024
  • CNS infection rate unchanged — hard floor of irreducible risk
  • Cerner imaging data will reveal subclinical disease — fills key gap
  • Confirmed discharge diagnoses from Cerner strengthen diagnostic accuracy
Manuscript Structure
SECTION 01

Introduction

Neurological complications of HIV arise from opportunistic infection, neoplasia, and direct viral effects. Despite the ART era, neurological disease remains a significant burden in HIV-positive inpatients.

The 2013 baseline audit (n=73) was conducted by a separate researcher — now a consultant neurologist at UCLH Queen's Square — who has since become the supervising consultant for this project. The 2024 audit (n=51) was conducted at the same ward using the same methodology. Both cohorts are from Ron Johnson Ward (RJW), the specialist HIV inpatient ward within Chelsea & Westminster Hospital. We now supplement the 2024 dataset with Cerner electronic records to add imaging results and confirmed discharge diagnoses.

One-line pitch: "ART has reduced symptom burden, but CNS infection admissions and neurology referrals remain unchanged — and uncontrolled viraemia drives virtually all neurological presentations in 2024."
SECTION 02

Methods

Design: Prospective observational audit, replicated methodology (2013 → 2024), supplemented with Cerner data (imaging + confirmed diagnoses).

Setting: Ron Johnson Ward (RJW) — specialist HIV inpatient ward within Chelsea & Westminster Hospital, London.

Analysis: Descriptive statistics. Chi-square for categorical. Mann-Whitney U for continuous. p<0.05 significant.

SECTION 03

Results

Patients older (52 vs 44), more ART-experienced (7 vs 4 yrs), but lower current CD4 (123 vs 366 cells/μL). Major reduction in symptoms (31% vs 73%, p<0.0001) and signs (29% vs 60%). CNS infections numerically lower but not statistically significant. 81% of symptomatic patients had detectable VL.

SECTION 04

Discussion

ART success story — but incomplete. Residual burden concentrated in those not virally suppressed. CNS infection admission rate unchanged (p=0.469). Stable neurology referral rate despite fewer symptoms may reflect heightened physician awareness.

Limitations: Single-centre, single-ward (RJW at CWH). Inpatient only. Small n (51 in 2024).

SECTION 05

Conclusion

Vigilance must be maintained — particularly for patients with uncontrolled viraemia. CNS infections persist. Neurological liaison services must be included in service development for HIV populations.

Target journals: HIV Medicine · JNNP · AIDS · The Lancet HIV (stretch)

なぜACFなのか
キャリア設計の観点から

IMT終了後に別途neurology ST3応募をするより、ACFとしてneurology trainingに直接入る方が、研究時間・場所選択・トレーニングポストを同時に確保できる。研究者＋臨床医のデュアルキャリアを最初から設計できる唯一のルート。
